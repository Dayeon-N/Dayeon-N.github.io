CHAPTER 8.2: E-SAFETY

Personal Data

  • Personal data refers to any data concerning a living person who can be identified either from the data itself or from the data in conjunction with other information
  • Examples:
    • Name
    • Address
    • Date of birth
    • Medical History
    • Banking details

Sensitive Personal Data

Includes:

  • Ethnic origin
  • Political views
  • Religion
  • Sexual orientation
  • Criminal activity

Definition of E-safety

E-safety refers to safety when using the internet

E-Safety Issues that can be encountered by users

  • Don’t give out any personal information to people who are unknown to you
  • Don’t send people photos of yourself
  • Always maintain your privacy settings on whatever device is being used online or during communications
    • Privacy settings allow the user to control which cookies are stored on their computer or enable the user to decide who can view certain information
  • When accessing the internet, make sure the websites being visited can be trusted
    • HTTPS or the padlock sign
    • When using search engines, always make sure the device settings are set to ‘safe search’ and the highest possible level of security is used
  • Only use websites recommended by teachers and only use a learner-friendly search engine
  • Only open emails from known sources
    • Make sure ISP has an effective email filtering feature to ensure unknown emails are placed in the spam box
  • Be vigilant when using social networking sites
    • Block or report anybody who acts suspiciously or who uses inappropriate language
    • Be very careful with the language used in chat rooms
    • Always use a nickname
    • Keep private and personal data secret
    • Don’t enter private chat rooms - stay public
    • Never arrange to meet anyone for the first time on your own
    • Always tell an adult first and meet the person in a public place
    • Avoid the misuse of images
    • Always use appropriate language
    • Always respect people’s confidentiality

Potential Risks When Playing Online Games

  • Violence in the game itself - can lead to violent behaviour in reality
  • Predators (people who prey on others who they see as vulnerable)
  • Cyberbullying
  • Use of webcams
  • Voice-masking technology (to disguise a voice so you can’t tell their sex or age, or even their accent)
  • Cyberattacks
    • Viruses
    • Phishing
    • Spyware
